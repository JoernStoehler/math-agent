\section*{Analysis 1 - Sheet 1, Exercise 2}

Let $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be defined by
$$f(x) = \begin{cases}
x^2 & \text{if } x \geq 0 \\
-x & \text{if } x < 0
\end{cases}$$

a) Show that $f$ is continuous at $x = 0$.

b) Is $f$ differentiable at $x = 0$? Justify your answer.
